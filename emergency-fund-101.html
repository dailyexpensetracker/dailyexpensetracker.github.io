Emergency Fund 101: Starting from Zero Dollars

The first step in any financial journey isn't investing—it's safety. An emergency fund is the buffer that prevents a car repair or a medical bill from turning into high-interest credit card debt.

Start small. Your first goal should be $1,000. It sounds impossible when you're living paycheck to paycheck, but using a tracker to find just $33 a day for one month will get you there.
