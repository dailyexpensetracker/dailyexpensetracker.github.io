The Dopamine of Spending vs. The Peace of Saving

Why is it so hard to say no to that shiny new gadget? The answer lies deep within your brain's reward system. Modern marketing is expertly tuned to trigger a surge of dopamine—the "anticipation" chemical—whenever we consider a purchase.

However, the dopamine hit is short-lived. It often leads to "buyer's remorse," a crash in mood that follows the temporary high of acquisition. In contrast, the peace of saving provides a long-term, stable state of serotonin-based satisfaction.
