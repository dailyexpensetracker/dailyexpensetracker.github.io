Mastering the 'Life Energy' Strategy: Why Your Time is More Valuable Than Your Money

728 x 90 Inline Ad

Most people track money as a number. But money is actually stored life energy. Every dollar you spend represents a fraction of your finite life that you traded at a job to earn that dollar.

In his seminal work, Joe Dominguez popularized the concept of "Life Energy." He argued that when we go to work, we are literally selling our life force for a paycheck. Therefore, when we buy something, we aren't just paying with currency; we're paying with the hours we spent sitting in an office or working on a project.

The True Hourly Wage

To master this strategy, you must calculate your *True Hourly Wage*. This isn't just your gross salary divided by hours. You must subtract the costs of working: transportation, work clothes, de-stressing after work, and the taxes taken from your check.

If your gross pay is $40/hour, your true hourly wage might be closer to $25. Now, look at that $150 dinner. It's not $150; it's **6 hours** of your life energy. Is that meal worth 6 hours of sitting under fluorescent lights? Sometimes the answer is yes, and sometimes it's a hard no.

Changing the Conversation

When you start seeing prices as hours, your relationship with spending changes forever. You stop asking "Do I have enough money?" and start asking "Is this item worth two days of my life?"

This awareness is the ultimate filter for consumerism. It allows you to decouple your happiness from "stuff" and re-align it with the value of your own time.
